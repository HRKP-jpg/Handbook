Welcome to KeyPlayers

Welcome to KeyPlayers — where people are our greatest asset. This handbook serves as your complete guide to company policies, expectations, and standards. It is designed to promote professionalism, accountability, and consistency across all Virtual Assistants (VAs).

All team members are expected to read, understand, and comply with the policies outlined in this handbook.

1
Purpose and Scope

This handbook applies to all KeyPlayers Virtual Assistants, employees, and contractors.

Our Goals

  • Maintain a professional and positive work culture
  • Ensure clear communication across teams
  • Implement fair and consistent practices

Core Principles

All VAs must:

  • Protect client and company data
  • Safeguard devices and login credentials
  • Follow secure and cloud-based workflows
  • Maintain professionalism, honesty, and integrity
  • Comply with both KeyPlayers and client policies

Pre-Day-1 Requirements

1. Bank Information Form

  • Fill out the provided bank form accurately
  • Ensure bank details are updated when changes occur

2. Scrin.io Setup (Time Tracking)

  • Accept your Scrin.io invite before Day 1
  • Scrin.io must remain active during all work hours
Pausing, blocking, or disabling Scrin.io is strictly prohibited.

3. Required Security Tools

Install and maintain: full disk encryption and anti-malware/anti-virus software.

4. Device Encryption (Mandatory)

Full-disk encryption is required at all times:

  • Windows — BitLocker or VeraCrypt
  • Mac — FileVault
Encryption must remain ON during employment.

5. Malware and Virus Protection

Required: Malwarebytes and Windows Defender or macOS XProtect.

If alerts appear: stop working and notify HR immediately.

6. Password Manager

Store passwords ONLY in LastPass or 1Password.

Not allowed: browser-saved passwords, notes apps, spreadsheets, or chat apps.

7. Secure Workspace Setup

Before starting work, ensure you have:

  • A private workspace
  • A secure internet connection (no public WiFi)
  • No one else uses your device
  • Screen not visible to others
4
Communication Standards & Third-Party Tools

Communication Standards

WhatsApp — Use for quick communication. Check messages often and reply promptly.

Slack — Our main communication tool. Check it regularly during your shift, respond promptly, and keep your status updated.

Email Etiquette — Acknowledge emails within 5 minutes. If you can't reply fully yet, send a short acknowledgment and follow up soon. Always use a professional and polite tone.

Third-Party Tools Policy

Prohibited Tools: Mouse movers, activity simulators, auto-clickers, time-manipulation apps.

Why: They misrepresent work activity, violate client trust, and create legal/compliance risks.

Consequences: Investigation, disciplinary action, and possible termination.

Professional Conduct & Ethics

All employees are expected to demonstrate professionalism in every interaction.

  • Avoid foul language, offensive jokes, or unprofessional comments
  • Treat colleagues, clients, and management with respect, honesty, and courtesy
  • Represent KeyPlayers with integrity and accountability at all times
6
Confidentiality & Data Security

Protecting client and company information is a fundamental responsibility. All data must be treated as confidential and handled with the highest level of care at all times.

General Guidelines

  • Never share passwords, files, or confidential information without proper authorization
  • Always log out of systems and tools when they are not in use
  • Immediately report any unusual or suspicious activity to HR or your Team Leader
Do's: Use only company-approved tools and platforms. Store files exclusively in the client's designated cloud storage. Report any security concerns immediately.
Don'ts: Do not use personal devices or storage for work files. Do not disclose client information to unauthorized persons. Do not capture or send screenshots containing sensitive data.
7
Employment and Probation

All newly hired Virtual Assistants are subject to a three-month probationary period.

During Probation

  • Performance, attendance, and workplace behavior are closely monitored and evaluated on an ongoing basis
  • The "No Work, No Pay" policy is strictly implemented, including during statutory holidays
  • Employees who are on probationary status are not yet eligible for paid statutory holidays
Important: All payments are processed under KeyPlayers. During probation, leaves are unpaid. Do not initiate or request paid leave during probation.

Regularization Policy

Regularization is determined on a per-client basis. A VA becomes regular after successfully completing the required probationary period with a specific client. When assigned to a new client, VAs will automatically return to probationary status for that new engagement.

  • Regularization is client-specific, not contract-based
  • A VA will undergo a probationary period for each new client assignment
  • Being a regular VA with one client does not carry over to another
8
Attendance & Time-Off Policy

Leave Credits (Annual Allocation)

  • 10 Vacation Leave Days
  • 5 Sick Leave Days
  • 5 Emergency Leave Days
Emergency leave is strictly for outages or urgent unforeseen situations only. Must be supported with valid documentation and is subject to HR review and approval.

Holiday Observance

  • We do not observe Philippine holidays
  • VAs are expected to work on North American statutory holidays by default, unless the client advises otherwise
  • The only exceptions are New Year's Day and Christmas Day, which are automatic days off

Statutory Holiday Policy

If a client does not require a VA to work on a statutory holiday, the VA must still submit a leave request. For regular VAs, approved statutory holidays are treated as paid time off. For probationary VAs, statutory holidays are unpaid unless stated otherwise by the client.

Planned Leave

  • 1–2 days: Request 2 weeks in advance
  • 3+ days: Request 1 month in advance
  • Notify HR and your Team Leader via email
  • Send a Slack or WhatsApp message for visibility
All leave requests must be submitted through the official Leave Request Form. Requests will not be considered valid without a completed form.

Unplanned Leave

  • Notify HR or your Team Leader at least 2 hours before your shift
  • A follow-up email is required for documentation
  • The Leave Request Form must still be completed after notification
No Call / No Show (NCNS): Strict zero-tolerance policy. May result in disciplinary action or termination.
9
Work Hours & Break Policy

Breaks are assigned based on shift duration:

  • 2–3 hour shift: No break
  • 4–6 hour shift: 15-minute break
  • 8 hour shift: Two (2) 15-minute breaks and one (1) 30-minute lunch break
Breaks must be managed responsibly and should not affect productivity.

Attendance & Performance Management

Work Readiness

  • Log in and be ready to work at your scheduled start time every day
  • Inform HR or your Supervisor in advance if you'll be late or unable to report
  • Report any unplanned absences as soon as possible through Slack, WhatsApp, or email
  • Repeated tardiness or NCNS incidents may lead to disciplinary action

Growth & Development

  • Embrace Feedback: Stay open to constructive feedback and apply it to improve your performance
  • Continuous Learning: Take initiative to learn new tools and skills
  • Be Proactive: Anticipate needs, take initiative, and manage your time effectively

Disciplinary Framework

KeyPlayers follows a progressive discipline policy:

  • Coaching — Verbal discussion and support for improvement
  • Written Warning — Formal notice documenting the issue
  • Final Written Warning — Issued for repeated or serious violations
  • Termination — Final step for continued non-compliance

Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

If performance issues are identified, the employee will be placed on a two-week PIP. Failure to show improvement may result in reassignment or termination.

11
Resignation Policy
  • Submit a formal resignation notice at least two (2) weeks before your final day
  • All handovers, access removals, and documentation must be completed before departure
  • Knowledge Transfer and Training Manuals must be submitted before final pay release
Failure to comply may delay or void the release of final payment.
12
Training Policy
  • All trainees are entitled to paid training, generally two (2) hours per day for up to two (2) weeks
  • Training payment will be released after successful client placement
  • The training covers system familiarization, process updates, and performance monitoring

Time Tracking & Payment (Scrin.io)

Scrin.io is the tool we use to track work hours. Please follow these guidelines:

General Rules

  • Scrin.io must run throughout your shift
  • Only tracked hours are paid
  • Logging out during shift may result in deductions and infractions
Important System Reminder: Do not leave your screen idle for long periods. Ensure your device stays active during your shift. Be responsible for monitoring your tracking tool. Scrin.io may log out automatically if your screen goes idle.

Screenshots & Notes

  • Do not delete or alter screenshots
  • Keep notes clear and professional

If Issues Occur

  • Report issues immediately
  • Share screenshots if possible
  • Wait for guidance from your Team Leader or HR
Following this policy helps protect both you and the company and ensures accurate pay.
